What if you don't get into medical school?

May 5, 2022 by Apply Medic

It is very important that you demonstrate that you have a very mature perspective on this issue. Medical school is incredibly competitive, with several students applying for every place in medical school. Therefore, it is important to have:

  • Sensible expectations during your application cycle

  • A back-up plan in place in case you are unsuccessful during this application cycle

If unsuccessful in your application, you must first use this time to reflect. Do you still want to pursue a career in medicine? If so, then you must focus on identifying what you can do to improve your future re-application. You can do this by reflecting on your own, or contacting the medical schools for feedback on your application.

Next, you need to think about what you will do until you re-apply. You could take a year out of full-time education to focus on gaining more work experience and gaining more insight into healthcare by getting a part-time job in non-clinical roles like healthcare assistant or hospital porter. Additionally, you could also study another honours degree like biomedical science at university, then re-apply to medicine in 3-4 years as a graduate to either undergraduate or post-graduate medicine programmes.

These are just a few possible options, and each have their pros and cons. It is therefore important to reflect and decide on what back-up plan is best for you. Best of luck in your interviews!

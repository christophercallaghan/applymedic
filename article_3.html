What are the qualities of a good doctor?

June 25, 2022 by Apply Medic

One of the most common medical school interview questions relates to what qualities you have that will make you a good doctor. This seems like a simple question from the outset, but becomes more complex the more you think about it. What is a quality? What is it that makes a good doctor? Why is growth mindset one of the most important of these qualities? We will answer all these questions in this article.

What is a personal quality?

A personal quality is an aspect of your personality. It relates to who you are as a person, how you interact with yourself, and how you interact with others.

What is it that makes a good doctor?

There are so many qualities that a person needs to be a good doctor. These might include good communication skills to relay information to patients and colleagues, empathy to begin to appreciate the perspective of the patient, and commitment to endure a long training pathway to become the doctor you want to be. However, there is one quality that I think is undervalued in medicine, and thatâ€™s having a growth mindset.

Why is a growth mindset important in medicine?

Human beings have an innate drive to grow and develop. A growth mindset allows people to see almost any skill as trainable and see themselves as full of potential that can be reached with active input. This therefore allows for a love of constant learning and development over time to become more competent as a human being. A fixed mindset, however, views themselves as having been born with talents that cannot be trained or developed, and therefore views others as simply having more innate talent than them. This is somewhat of an oversimplification of these concepts. However, it gets the point across that as a doctor, you develop an array of skills that develop over many years, and will be continually learning in medical school, during post-graduate training, and even as a consultant. So, to really love and enjoy medicine, a growth mindset is essential.
